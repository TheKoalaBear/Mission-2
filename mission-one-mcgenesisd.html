Kyoto, Japan

Kiyozumi Dera

Kiyomizu-dera is a Buddhist temple located in eastern Kyoto, Japan. The temple is part of the Historic Monuments of Ancient Kyoto UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Kyoto, Japan

Golden Pavilion

Kinkaku-ji, officially named Rokuon-ji, is a Zen Buddhist temple in Kyoto, Japan. It is one of the most popular buildings in Kyoto, attracting many visitors annually.

Shibuya, Japan

Shibuya Crossing

Shibuya Scramble Crossing, commonly known as Shibuya Crossing, is a popular pedestrian scramble crossing in Shibuya, Tokyo, Japan. It is located in front of the Shibuya Station Hachikō exit and stops vehicles in all directions to allow pedestrians to inundate the entire intersection.

Tokyo, Japan

Sakura Festival

Japan's cherry blossom festivals are a centuries-old cultural tradition. Taking place throughout the entire country, these festivals are commemorated with picnics, parties, sake, and viewing parties, also referred to as hanami, which means “viewing flowers.”

Kyoto, Japan

Inari Shrine

Fushimi Inari Taisha is a Shinto shrine in Kyoto, Japan. It is seen as the principal residence of the kami Inari, Shinto god of rice and commerce. The shrine was built around 711 CE, and grew in size and importance over time, even receiving a torii gate from Toyotomi Hideyoshi in 1589.
